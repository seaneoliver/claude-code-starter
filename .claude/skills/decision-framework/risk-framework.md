# Risk Framework

## For Each Major Risk

**Risk:** [What could go wrong]

**Likelihood:** High / Medium / Low
- High: >50% chance
- Medium: 20-50% chance
- Low: <20% chance

**Impact:** High / Medium / Low
- High: Significant business impact, hard to recover
- Medium: Moderate impact, recoverable
- Low: Minor inconvenience

**Priority:** Likelihood x Impact
- High/High = Top priority to mitigate
- High/Medium or Medium/High = Important to address
- Low/Low = Monitor, don't over-invest

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Reduce likelihood: [How to make it less likely]
- Reduce impact: [How to limit damage if it happens]
- Contingency plan: [What to do if it happens anyway]

---

## Risk Categories

**Execution Risk**
We can't deliver what we planned. Common signals: dependencies on others, new technology, tight timelines, insufficient resources.

**Adoption Risk**
Stakeholders don't use the solution. Workarounds continue. Change resistance or poor communication drives this.

**Quality Risk**
Solution doesn't work as intended. Creates new problems or misses requirements. Often surfaces from insufficient testing or unclear specs.

**Relationship Risk**
Damages stakeholder relationships. Creates political issues. Loses trust. Often the most underweighted risk in analytical frameworks.

**Strategic Risk**
Doesn't align with org direction. Becomes obsolete quickly. Opportunity cost is too high — we built the right thing for the wrong moment.
