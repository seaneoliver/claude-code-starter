# Decision Analysis Procedure

## 6-Step Structure

### Step 1: Frame the Decision

Write a decision statement in this form:

```
We need to decide [specific choice] by [date] because [why it matters].

If we don't decide:
- [Consequence 1]
- [Consequence 2]

Success looks like:
- [Outcome 1]
- [Outcome 2]
```

Capture: what the decision is, when it must be made, and what inaction costs.

---

### Step 2: Identify Options

Minimum 3 options. Structure:

- Option A: Bold change
- Option B: Moderate change
- Option C: Status quo or minimal change

Three options avoids false binaries, shows exploration, and gives the decision-maker a real choice.

---

### Step 3: Evaluation Criteria

Define criteria before analyzing options. Common criteria:

- Business Impact: Value delivered
- Stakeholder Satisfaction: User acceptance
- Feasibility: Can we actually do this?
- Risk: What could go wrong?
- Cost: Time, money, effort
- Scalability: Does it grow with us?
- Timeline: How fast can we implement?

Weight each criterion:
- Critical (must-have)
- Important (should-have)
- Nice-to-have

---

### Step 4: Options Analysis

For each option, capture:

**Pros:**
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]

**Cons:**
- [Drawback 1]
- [Drawback 2]

**Risks:**
- [Risk] - Likelihood: H/M/L - Impact: H/M/L - Mitigation: [approach]

**Cost/Effort:**
- Time: [estimate]
- Resources: [what's needed]
- Opportunity cost: [what we won't do]

**Stakeholder Impact:**
- Who wins: [stakeholders who benefit]
- Who's inconvenienced: [stakeholders affected]

---

### Step 5: Comparison Matrix

Score each option 1-10 per criterion. Weight by importance (Critical > Important > Nice-to-have).

| Criteria | Weight | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|----------|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Business Impact | Critical | score | score | score |
| Stakeholder Satisfaction | Critical | score | score | score |
| Feasibility | Important | score | score | score |
| Risk | Important | score | score | score |
| Timeline | Nice-to-have | score | score | score |
| **Total** | | | | |

Scoring guidance:
- 9-10: Strongly meets this criterion
- 6-8: Moderately meets it
- 3-5: Partially meets it
- 1-2: Fails to meet it

The matrix surfaces trade-offs visually and forces explicit scoring before recommendation.

---

### Step 6: Recommendation

Structure:

**My Recommendation:** [Option X]

**Rationale:**
- [Reason tied to criteria]
- [Reason tied to business outcome]
- [Reason that addresses key concern]

**Why Not Other Options:**
- Option Y: [Why it falls short]
- Option Z: [Why it's not optimal]

**Implementation Approach:**
- [Key step 1]
- [Key step 2]
- [Key step 3]

**Success Metrics:**
- [How we'll know it worked]
- [Timeline to measure]

Never end with "What do you think?" — provide a clear point of view and ask for validation, not delegation.
