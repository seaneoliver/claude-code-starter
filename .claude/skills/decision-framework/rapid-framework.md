# RAPID Framework

Microsoft's standard for clarifying decision rights.

## Roles

**R - Recommend:** Who proposes the decision. Usually you.

**A - Approve:** Who has final say. Your manager or relevant leadership.

**P - Perform:** Who implements the decision once made.

**I - Input:** Who provides data, perspective, or expertise. Consulted but not deciding.

**D - Decide:** Who makes the call. Sometimes the same person as Approve; sometimes different.

---

## Why It Matters

Skipping RAPID role identification causes:
- Endless discussion with no decision (no one knows who has D)
- Rework because the wrong person decided (A was bypassed)
- Resentment when stakeholders feel excluded (I was skipped)

Naming the roles upfront makes the process faster and the outcome more defensible.

---

## How to Identify Roles for a Decision

Work through these questions:

1. Who will make the final call? That's D (and often also A).
2. Is there a separate approval step above that? If so, A is distinct from D.
3. Who is proposing and structuring the recommendation? That's R.
4. Who will do the work once decided? That's P.
5. Who has relevant expertise, data, or a stake in the outcome? Those are the I roles.

Document the roles explicitly in the decision record before presenting the recommendation.
