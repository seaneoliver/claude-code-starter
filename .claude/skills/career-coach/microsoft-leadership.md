# Microsoft Leadership Principles

Microsoft leaders do three things: **Create Clarity, Generate Energy, and Deliver Success**. Everyone has the potential to be a leader at Microsoft — including an {{YOUR_LEVEL}} Business Analytics Lead.

---

## Leadership Principle 1: Create Clarity

**We simplify complexity to ensure shared understanding.**

### Core Behaviors
1. **Synthesize the complex** — Distill complex data, analyses, and business challenges into understandable insights
2. **Ensure shared understanding** — Align stakeholders on what the data means and what actions to take
3. **Define a course of action** — Translate insights into clear next steps

### At Our Best ({{YOUR_LEVEL}}/{{YOUR_NEXT_LEVEL}} Application)
- Make time to reflect on the environment before diving into analysis
- Consider external market trends and how they impact internal data
- Seek out diverse viewpoints when interpreting data
- Ask stakeholders what they see in the same metrics
- Partner with teams who have different business context
- Ensure stakeholders agree on what problem we're solving
- Build consensus on evaluation frameworks and success metrics
- Gather feedback as analysis progresses and adapt approach

### Not at Our Best (Avoid These)
- Rush to analysis without understanding the business question
- Present data without ensuring stakeholders understand it the same way
- "Sell" your analytical approach without listening to concerns
- Agree to an approach in meetings but do something different

### {{YOUR_LEVEL}} Examples
- Synthesized complex ROB performance data into 3 key insights for leadership review
- Facilitated alignment between Finance and Sales on definition of pipeline metrics
- Translated technical data quality issues into business impact for stakeholders
- Defined evaluation framework for new initiative with cross-functional team

### {{YOUR_NEXT_LEVEL}} Examples
- Led organization-wide alignment on preferred evaluation methodology for product launches
- Synthesized insights from multiple business areas to define strategic course of action
- Established shared understanding across leadership of external market trends impacting internal metrics
- Guided teams through complex analytical challenges to ensure shared vision

### Self-Reflection
- [ ] Do I simplify complexity or add to it with my analysis?
- [ ] Do I ensure stakeholders have shared understanding before moving forward?
- [ ] Do I listen and adjust my approach based on feedback?
- [ ] Do I define clear courses of action from my insights?

## Leadership Principle 2: Generate Energy

**We inspire optimism, creativity, commitment, and growth.**

### Core Behaviors
1. **Inspire optimism, creativity, and growth** — Show possibilities, not just problems
2. **Create an environment where everyone does their best work** — Enable and empower others
3. **Build teams that are stronger tomorrow than today** — Develop capability and maturity

### At Our Best ({{YOUR_LEVEL}}/{{YOUR_NEXT_LEVEL}} Application)
- Recognize how your insights and communication style affect stakeholder energy
- Acknowledge contributions from data providers, engineers, and partners
- Bring positive energy to analytical challenges
- Present data challenges as opportunities to improve
- Show paths forward even when metrics are unfavorable
- Balance critique with possibility (what we can do)
- Deliver immediate analytical needs while building scalable solutions
- Create reusable frameworks while addressing urgent requests
- Invest in capability-building while meeting deadlines

### Not at Our Best (Avoid These)
- Focus only on problems in the data without solutions
- Wait for direction instead of proactively identifying opportunities
- Turn presentations into complaint sessions about data quality
- Focus on short-term deliverables at expense of team burnout

### {{YOUR_LEVEL}} Examples
- Turned data quality challenge into opportunity to improve pipeline with engineering
- Mentored colleague on analytical technique, raising their capability
- Presented unfavorable metrics with actionable path forward, inspiring team action
- Built reusable dashboard that reduced manual work, enabling team to focus on insights

### {{YOUR_NEXT_LEVEL}} Examples
- Led team through complex analytical challenge, building their skills and confidence
- Infused organization with optimism about analytics capabilities through thought leadership
- Balanced immediate business needs with building scalable analytics infrastructure
- Created environment where analysts experiment and grow, strengthening team capability

### Self-Reflection
- [ ] Do I inspire optimism or focus mainly on problems?
- [ ] Do I enable others to do their best work?
- [ ] Am I building team capability for the future?
- [ ] Do I balance short-term needs with long-term sustainability?

## Leadership Principle 3: Deliver Success

**We ensure the right tools are in place to drive innovation.**

### Core Behaviors
1. **Drive innovation that people love** — Create solutions stakeholders want to use
2. **Be boundary-less in seeking solutions** — Look beyond your immediate team/tools
3. **Tenaciously pursue the right outcomes** — Focus on impact, not just completion

### At Our Best ({{YOUR_LEVEL}}/{{YOUR_NEXT_LEVEL}} Application)
- Envision analytics capabilities beyond current state
- Imagine how AI/automation could transform reporting
- Consider what insights could unlock if data barriers were removed
- Investigate data quality issues to understand root causes
- Explore how analytical challenges could improve processes
- Turn stakeholder questions into opportunities to add value
- Share your analytical thinking process with others
- Document approaches so others can learn and reuse

### Not at Our Best (Avoid These)
- Ignore stakeholder feedback because of technical constraints
- Take action without thinking through risks
- Reward activity (number of reports) over impact (decisions influenced)

### {{YOUR_LEVEL}} Examples
- Drove adoption of self-service dashboard loved by stakeholders for its usability
- Looked beyond Power BI to integrate external data sources for comprehensive view
- Tenaciously pursued data quality improvements until stakeholders trusted the metrics
- Evaluated AI automation opportunity and implemented solution reducing manual work

### {{YOUR_NEXT_LEVEL}} Examples
- Drove organization-wide innovation in analytics methodology adopted across teams
- Sought boundary-less solutions across Microsoft, connecting your business to other areas
- Tenaciously pursued strategic outcomes, overcoming multiple barriers to deliver impact
- Partnered with Engineering/Data Science to develop automated solutions at scale

### Self-Reflection
- [ ] Do I drive innovation stakeholders love?
- [ ] Do I look beyond my immediate tools/team for solutions?
- [ ] Do I pursue the right outcomes or just check boxes?
- [ ] Do I bring others along the learning journey?

## Leadership in Daily Analytics Work

**Morning Review (Create Clarity):**
- Review stakeholder requests: What's the real business question?
- Synthesize overnight alerts/metrics: What's the simple story?
- Plan your day: What's the course of action?

**Analytical Work (All Three Principles):**
- Create Clarity: Simplify complex data into understandable insights
- Generate Energy: Show possibilities, not just problems
- Deliver Success: Focus on impact, not just completion

**Stakeholder Meetings (Create Clarity + Generate Energy):**
- Ensure shared understanding before moving forward
- Inspire optimism with paths forward
- Balance candor about challenges with energy about solutions

**Deliverables (Deliver Success):**
- Drive innovation stakeholders love (self-service, intuitive)
- Ensure tools are in place (reusable, discoverable)
- Pursue right outcomes (influence decisions, not just report metrics)
